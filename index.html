About Me

I bring a dynamic blend of positivity, dedication, and responsibility to the academic environment. With extensive Lecturing experience, I am passionate about both the art of Lecturing and the journey of learning, sharing knowledge in engineering processes, electrochemistry, physical sciences, and mathematics. My approach extends beyond academics, as I actively address students' psychosocial needs, empowering them to grow and succeed holistically. Committed to excellence, I inspire my students and colleagues alike, fostering a collaborative environment where everyone strives to achieve their best.

Materials scientist and Associate Professor with 15+ years' experience developing nanocomposites for cancer therapeutics and sustainable energy. Published 3+ high-impact papers on macromolecular drug delivery (avg. citations: 45). Elevated student pass rates by 20% through innovative STEM pedagogy. Expert in SEM/TEM characterization and MATLAB simulation.

Research Highlights

Fuel Cells: Ion-exchange Membranes

Developed novel hydrogen fuel cell membranes with 15% higher proton conductivity than commercial benchmarks.

Cancer Therapy: Drug Delivery Systems

Designed ferrocene-methotrexate conjugates showing 3x tumor suppression in murine models.

Materials: Nanocomposites

Engineered styrene-butadiene rubber variants for enhanced fuel cell performance (2 patents pending).
